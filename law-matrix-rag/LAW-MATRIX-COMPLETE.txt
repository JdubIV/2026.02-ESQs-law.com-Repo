SECTION 2: GENERAL FORMATTING RULES
================================================================================

All Utah court filings MUST comply with these formatting rules unless court-specific
rules override them.

PAGE SETUP:
-----------
- Paper Size: Letter (8.5 x 11 inches)
- Top Margin: 1.00 inch (CRITICAL: NOT the Word default of 1.50 inch)
- Bottom Margin: 1.00 inch
- Left Margin: 1.00 inch
- Right Margin: 1.00 inch

FONT:
-----
- Family: Times New Roman
- Size: 12pt
- Use throughout entire document (no mixed fonts)

LINE SPACING:
-------------
- Body text / paragraphs: Double-spaced (2.0)
- Attorney header block: Single-spaced (1.0)
- Table content: Single-spaced (1.0)
- Certificate of Service body: Double-spaced (2.0)

PARAGRAPH FORMATTING:
---------------------
- First-line indent: 0.5 inch for body paragraphs
- Alignment: Left-justified for body text
- Center alignment: Document titles, Certificate of Service heading

PARAGRAPH NUMBERING:
--------------------
- Sequential numbering (1, 2, 3...) for regular paragraphs ONLY
- Do NOT number: headers, sub-headers, or list items within numbered paragraphs
- If a numbered paragraph contains a list (a), (b), (c), those list items do NOT
  get separate paragraph numbers
- Sub-paragraphs use decimal notation: 1.1, 1.2, 2.1, 2.2, etc.

ATTORNEY HEADER FORMAT (URCP 10(a)(3)):
----------------------------------------
Single-spaced, placed at top of first page as standard paragraphs (NOT in a table).

CASE CAPTION:
-------------
- Must be in TABLE format
- 2 columns, 2 rows (NOT 3 columns)
- Right column cells merged into one cell spanning both rows
- Top-left cell: Court name (centered, single-spaced)
- Bottom-left cell: Party names (left-aligned, single-spaced)
- Right cell (merged): Document title, case number, judge name
- Borders: Outside borders only (2.25pt), inside vertical border, no horizontal
  inside borders
- Court name appears ONCE only (in top-left cell, never duplicated in right cell)
- PLAINTIFF and DEFENDANT labels in ALL CAPS

Caption content format:
  Top-left cell (centered):
    IN THE [ORDINAL] JUDICIAL DISTRICT COURT
    IN AND FOR [COUNTY] COUNTY, STATE OF UTAH

  Bottom-left cell (left-aligned):
    [PLAINTIFF/PETITIONER NAME],
              Plaintiff/Petitioner,

    v.

    [DEFENDANT/RESPONDENT NAME],
              Defendant/Respondent.

  Right cell (merged, left-aligned):
    [DOCUMENT TITLE]

    Case No.: [CASE NUMBER]
    Judge: [JUDGE NAME]

DOCUMENT TITLE FORMATTING:
--------------------------
- ALL CAPS for document titles in the caption
- Bold is optional depending on court preference
- Centered when standalone (outside caption table)

WARNING BOX (for motions only, NOT for responses):
--------------------------------------------------
Position: Top right corner of first page
Format: Bordered box with bold text
Text:
  This motion requires you to respond.
  Failure to respond within 14 days may result
  in the Court granting this motion without
  further notice.

PROPOSED ORDERS (Utah e-filing format):
---------------------------------------
- Electronic signature notation at top: Signed electronically pursuant to
  Utah R. Civ. P. 5(e).
- At bottom: APPROVED AS TO FORM: with attorney signature line
- No judge signature line at end (judges sign electronically at front via
  e-filing system)

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
-----------------------
- Incarcerated client: Filings under attorney authority per URCP Rule 11
- NO phantom law, cases, or facts: Every legal citation must be verifiable
- Request clarification for unclear information before proceeding

SECTION 3: UTAH RULES OF CIVIL PROCEDURE (URCP) REFERENCES
================================================================================

Key rules referenced in document preparation:

- URCP Rule 5(e): Electronic filing and service
- URCP Rule 10(a)(3): Format requirements for pleadings (attorney header)
- URCP Rule 11: Signing of pleadings, motions, and other papers; representations
  to court; sanctions
- URCP Rule 12(b): Defenses and objections (motions to dismiss)
  - 12(b)(1): Lack of subject matter jurisdiction
  - 12(b)(2): Lack of personal jurisdiction
  - 12(b)(3): Improper venue
  - 12(b)(4): Insufficient process
  - 12(b)(5): Insufficient service of process
  - 12(b)(6): Failure to state a claim upon which relief can be granted
  - 12(b)(7): Failure to join a party under Rule 19
- URCP Rule 26: Duty to disclose; general provisions governing discovery
- URCP Rule 33: Interrogatories to parties
- URCP Rule 34: Production of documents and things
- URCP Rule 36: Requests for admission
- URCP Rule 37: Failure to make disclosure or cooperate in discovery; sanctions
- URCP Rule 56: Summary judgment

4.1 NOTICE OF APPEARANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Document Type: Notice of Appearance
Format: Utah Court Standard
Court: First Judicial District, Cache County (default)
Expected Result: 1-page document with 3 tables total

STRUCTURE:
1. Attorney Header (paragraphs, single-spaced)
2. Caption Table (2 columns, right merged, borders)
3. Body Text (double-spaced, 0.5 inch first-line indent)
4. Date Line
5. Signature Table (3 columns, no borders, middle column empty)
6. Certificate of Service with Service Table (2 columns, borders)

REQUIRED FIELDS:
- caseNumber
- plaintiffName
- defendantName
- judgeName
- clientRole (Plaintiff/Defendant/Petitioner/Respondent)
- clientName

BODY TEXT (Paragraph 1):
Diane Pitcher (#12626), and John W Adams III (#19429) of the firm Pitcher Law
PLLC, hereby enter their notices of appearance on behalf of the [Plaintiff /
Defendant], [Party Name], in the above captioned case. [If Defendant add: To the
extent necessary, the Defendant enters a plea of not guilty and hereby gives
notice of his demand for a jury and speedy trial.]

BODY TEXT (Paragraph 2):
Please direct all future correspondence to the above address.

SIGNATURE TABLE LAYOUT:
- 3 columns x 3 rows, NO visible borders
- Column 1: Diane Pitcher signature and info
- Column 2: EMPTY (spacer)
- Column 3: John W. Adams III signature and info
- Row 1: /s/ signatures
- Row 2: Printed names
- Row 3: Attorneys for [Party]

Each signature appears ONCE only. Middle column stays empty in all rows.

DATE LINE FORMAT:
DATED this [___] day of [________], [____].

VALIDATION RULES:
- Top margin = 1.00 inch (not 1.50 inch)
- Caption table = 2 columns (not 3)
- Signature table = 3 columns (not 8)
- Service table = 2 columns (not 3)
- Total tables in document = EXACTLY 3
- Document fits on 1 page
- No duplicate text in table cells
- Court header appears in ONE cell only

--- 4.2.2 MOTION TO COMPEL DISCOVERY ---

Document Title: MOTION TO COMPEL DISCOVERY
Subtitle: Pursuant to Rule 37
Category: Motions

REQUIRED FIELDS:
- courtName, courtDistrict, courtState, courtCounty
- plaintiff, defendant, caseNumber, judgeName
- movant (Moving Party)
- respondent (Responding Party)
- discoveryType (what discovery is at issue)
- discoveryServed (date discovery was served)
- responsesDue (date responses were due)
- deficiencies (array of specific discovery deficiencies)
- meetConferDetails (meet and confer efforts description)

STRUCTURE:
MOTION TO COMPEL DISCOVERY RESPONSES

[Movant], by and through undersigned counsel, respectfully moves this Court for
an Order compelling [Respondent] to fully and completely respond to [Movant]'s
[Discovery Type], and in support thereof states:

INTRODUCTION
[Movant] served [Discovery Type] on [Respondent] on [Date Served]. Responses
were due on [Date Due]. Despite the passage of the deadline and good faith
efforts to meet and confer, [Respondent] has failed to provide complete and
adequate responses.

BACKGROUND
[Case background and relevance of discovery]

ARGUMENT
I. [RESPONDENT] HAS FAILED TO RESPOND TO DISCOVERY
[Respondent]'s responses are deficient in the following respects:
1. [Deficiency 1]
2. [Deficiency 2]

II. [MOVANT] HAS MET THE MEET AND CONFER REQUIREMENT
[Meet and confer details]

III. THIS COURT SHOULD COMPEL COMPLETE RESPONSES
[Legal argument for compelling discovery]

CONCLUSION
For the foregoing reasons, [Movant] respectfully requests that this Court enter
an Order:
1. Compelling [Respondent] to serve full and complete responses within 14 days;
2. Awarding [Movant] reasonable attorney fees and costs; and
3. Granting such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.

SECTION 6: EVAL / QUALITY RULES
================================================================================

Before delivering ANY document, run the following quality checks:

1. PARTY CHECK (pass/fail):
   - Do all names and roles match the party_cache?
   - Is the client name correct and in the right role?
   - Is the opposing party correctly identified?
   - HARD RULE: We ALWAYS represent the client_name. NEVER the opposing party.

2. FORMAT CHECK (pass/flag/fail):
   - Does output follow Law Matrix formatting rules?
   - Correct numbering scheme (sequential, not bullets)?
   - Correct font (Times New Roman 12pt)?
   - Correct margins (1.00 inch all sides)?
   - Correct line spacing (double for body, single for headers/tables)?
   - IRAC structure for legal arguments (Issue, Rule, Application, Conclusion)?
   - Court-specific formatting applied?

3. COMPLETENESS CHECK (pass/flag/fail):
   - Are all required sections present?
   - Are all required fields filled?
   - Is the signature block complete?
   - Is the Certificate of Service included?
   - Are all placeholder fields marked with brackets?

4. ACCURACY CHECK (pass/flag/fail):
   - Does content match local case data?
   - Are legal citations verifiable?
   - NO phantom law, cases, or facts
   - Are dates correct?
   - Are bar numbers correct?

OVERALL SCORING:
- All checks pass: Deliver document
- Any checks flagged: Deliver with disclaimer noting flagged items
- Any checks fail: Fix before delivering

Log every evaluation to the eval_log table for audit trail.
